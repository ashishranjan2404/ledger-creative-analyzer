## Part 1 — The 7-signal Archetype-5 detection checklist

Seven signals, memorizable as three categories: **initiative**, **specificity**, **friction-tolerance**. For each: what to listen for, what it weights toward, and the false-positive twin Ashish must distinguish it from.

### Signal 1 — Who raised Thedi first in the last two weeks

- **What to observe.** Scroll back on the text thread before coffee. Did *Ramesh* reference the newsletter/Substack/"the thing we talked about" unprompted? Or has Ashish been the one re-raising it since the original peer-nudge moment?
- **Archetype-5 weighting.** Ramesh re-raised → +Archetype 5 or 2. Ashish re-raised every time → +Archetype 1 or 4.
- **False-positive twin.** A polite "hey btw still down for coffee" reply to Ashish's ping is NOT Ramesh-initiated. It's reactive civility. Only count messages where Ramesh introduced a new thought about the project without being asked.
- **Hardness-to-fake.** High. Text history is a fixed artifact; no in-the-moment politeness can retcon it.

### Signal 2 — Specificity of what he wants from the Substack

- **What to observe.** When Ashish asks the North Star question ("which of the three 12-month outcomes makes you say this was worth it?"), does Ramesh answer with (a) a specific outcome and a specific reason, (b) "whichever you think is best," or (c) a hedge ("maybe both, let's see")?
- **Archetype-5 weighting.** (a) with named reason = Archetype 5 or 3. (b) = Archetype 1 or 4. (c) = Archetype 1 blended with 4.
- **False-positive twin.** "I want advisory engagements" *said confidently* is NOT specificity if he can't name *who* he'd advise or *on what*. Ask the follow-up: "Advising whom, on what?" A real Archetype-5 answer has a named vertical and a named problem. A polite-sounding answer evaporates under that follow-up.
- **Hardness-to-fake.** Medium-high. Hard to invent a named advisory market on the spot.

### Signal 3 — Whether he asks about the rubric, prompts, or models unprompted

- **What to observe.** During the 30 minutes, does Ramesh ask anything like "what models are you using?", "how does the critic decide?", "can I see the rubric?" — *without Ashish raising the pipeline first*?
- **Archetype-5 weighting.** Unprompted = Archetype 5 (co-design impulse) or Archetype 3 (audit impulse). Silent throughout = Archetype 1 or 4.
- **False-positive twin.** "Cool, what model is it?" asked once in passing IS default-engineer-curiosity, NOT co-design impulse. Distinguishing signal: does he ask a *second* question that builds on the first? Archetype 5 goes two levels deep. Archetype 3 asks one question and logs it. Archetype 1/4 doesn't ask.
- **Hardness-to-fake.** High for Archetype 5 specifically. Faking two levels of follow-up requires actually caring.

### Signal 4 — Reaction to the "it might be overkill" framing

- **What to observe.** Sometime in Act 2 or 3, Ashish says a version of *"honestly, this might be overkill for a newsletter — we could also just do a Friday digest and a Google Doc."* How does Ramesh respond?
- **Archetype-5 weighting.** Pushback ("no, I want the pipeline because [specific reason]") = Archetype 5. Relief ("oh, that actually sounds great") = Archetype 4 with honest surfacing, or Archetype 1. Polite neutrality ("you know best") = Archetype 1 or 3.
- **False-positive twin.** THE MOST IMPORTANT ONE. Ramesh saying *"no no, the pipeline sounds cool, let's do it"* is NOT pushback — it's politeness. Real Archetype-5 pushback has a **named specific blocker v0 creates for him**: "I won't write weekly if I don't have Socratic prompts pushing me," or "I want the rubric because I want to audit my own drift." Politeness has no specific blocker, just warm affirmation.
- **Hardness-to-fake.** VERY HIGH. This is the single most load-bearing signal in the whole diagnostic. Ashish's pattern-matcher will want to read polite affirmation as pushback. **If Ramesh can't name a concrete blocker v0 creates, his "I want v2" is not Archetype-5 signal.**

### Signal 5 — Prior public writing traces

- **What to observe.** Before coffee, Ashish spends 10 minutes checking: Ramesh's LinkedIn (posts? articles? comments on AI-safety threads?), any internal Saviynt tech-talk slides, any prior Substack/Medium that may exist under his name, any conference talks.
- **Archetype-5 weighting.** Active LinkedIn writing in the last 6 months with substantive content (not reshares) = Archetype 5 or 3. Dormant LinkedIn, no public writing = Archetype 1 or 4. A few cutting comments on AI-safety posts but no writing of his own = Archetype 3 specifically.
- **False-positive twin.** A single LinkedIn post from 14 months ago is NOT "active writer." Must be in the last 6 months and must be original, not a reshare-with-emoji.
- **Hardness-to-fake.** Completely unfakeable — it's either there or it isn't, and Ramesh doesn't know Ashish is looking.

### Signal 6 — How he handles the five structural conditions

- **What to observe.** In Act 2 of the iter-4 script, Ashish walks through the 5 points (OSS on personal GitHub, Ramesh self-hosts, no Saviynt-channel crossover, recusal protocol, revenue re-papering threshold). Does Ramesh (a) accept with specifics ("makes sense — should the recusal file cover skip-level only or also panel?"), (b) accept with polite vagueness ("yeah, whatever works"), or (c) push back with concerns that reveal engagement ("wait, $500/mo sustained is low — what counts as attributable revenue?")?
- **Archetype-5 weighting.** (a) or (c) with specifics = Archetype 5 or 3. (b) = Archetype 1, 4, or at best a distracted Archetype 5.
- **False-positive twin.** "Yes to all five" delivered quickly and warmly is NOT engagement-signal. It's compliance. Real engagement produces at least one clarifying question on at least one of the five points, because no competent engineer accepts five structural conditions without one edge case surfacing.
- **Hardness-to-fake.** Medium-high. Asking a clarifying question requires actually modeling the scenario.

### Signal 7 — How he handles the "no money, no Slack, RECUSAL" conditions specifically

- **What to observe.** This is Signal 6 narrowed to the three non-negotiables. When Ashish says "no money flows," does Ramesh (a) try once to offer payment ("let me just pay you for your time") — which is Appendix B in the iter-4 script — or (b) accept silently?
- **Archetype-5 weighting.** Trying to pay then accepting the OSS-framing explanation = Archetype 5 or 3 (both have the "I should compensate this" instinct). Silent acceptance = Archetype 1 or 4. Silent acceptance with visible relief = Archetype 1.
- **False-positive twin.** A theatrical "oh I'd totally pay you!" with no actual follow-through intent is NOT the signal. Real version: Ramesh names a specific amount or structure ("like, $2K for the build?") before Ashish declines. Specificity separates earnest offer from social-lubricant offer.
- **Hardness-to-fake.** High. Naming a specific dollar amount requires having actually thought about it.

### The counting rule

- **5+ signals pointing to Archetype 5** (with the false-positive twins ruled out) → **v2 from day 1** is defensible.
- **3–4 signals pointing to Archetype 5** → **v0 for 3 months, revisit**. The honest default per DECISION.md.
- **1–2 signals** → **v0 or Scout-digest-only** depending on Signal 2 (specificity of wanted outcome).
- **0 signals with visible relief at "just a Friday digest"** → **Scout-digest-only**.
- **0 signals with visible fatigue at the whole topic** → **Drop it entirely**.

**Signal 4 is the tie-breaker.** If Signal 4 is clearly positive (named blocker pushback), add a full signal-weight. If it's clearly negative (polite affirmation with no named blocker), subtract one. This is the single signal most resistant to pattern-matching error.

## Part 2 — The branching decision tree

### Branch 1 — v2 from day 1

**Trigger signals (exact phrases Ramesh might say).**
- *"I've actually been thinking about the prompt design — can we co-design the rubric?"*
- *"A Friday digest wouldn't get me to write weekly. I need the Socratic prompts."*
- *"Show me the critic dimensions before we start; I want to propose one."*

**What Ashish says at the end of coffee to confirm the branch.**
> *"Okay, I hear you — you want the full pipeline because [name his specific reason]. Let me say back what that means so we both know what we agreed: I'm filing the §2870 letter this week, we do the 30-minute seed interview the weekend after install, and I'm booking 7 Saturdays over the next 10 weeks for the build. If that shape still works for you after you sleep on it, reply 'confirmed' to the email thread by Wednesday. If it doesn't, just say so — no hard feelings, we fall back to the digest."*

**48-hour actions.**
1. Send the email-thread follow-up summarizing the 5 points plus the North Star sentence in Ramesh's own words (per iter-4 script exit).
2. Draft the §2870 letter for attorney review; do NOT file yet (wait for Ramesh's written "confirmed").
3. Book the install call calendar invite for ~2026-05-23 per iter-4 logistics.
4. Add `RECUSAL.md`, `README.md`, and `FROZEN_UNTIL_CARVEOUT.md` to a local branch but do not push.

**Written follow-up.** One email, three paragraphs: (1) summary of what was agreed with the verbatim North Star sentence, (2) the 5 points with "confirmed in call on 2026-04-XX" next to each, (3) explicit ask: *"Reply 'confirmed' by Wednesday. If any of this drifted from what you heard, flag it."*

### Branch 2 — v0 for 3 months, revisit (DEFAULT)

**Trigger signals (exact phrases).**
- *"Yeah, all that sounds fine — you know best."*
- *"Let's just start and see what happens."*
- *"I want to write, I just don't have a clear picture of what."*

**What Ashish says at the end of coffee to confirm the branch.**

You tag me, I give you an editor's pass within 48 hours, you publish on Substack. That's the whole thing for the next 90 days — no pipeline, no rubric, no LLM touching your prose. If at day 90 you're hitting a wall that automation would fix, tell me what the wall is and I'll build the pipeline then. If you're shipping fine, we keep going as-is. Sound right?"*

**48-hour actions.**
1. Re-target the v1 scout to send the Friday digest to Ramesh's inbox; set Ashish bcc for QA (per iter-8 setup table).
2. Create the `thedi-drafts/` Google Drive folder with the template doc; share edit access with Ramesh.
3. Send the v0-README (6-line contract) via email.
4. File the §2870 letter anyway — it's cheap and future-proofs if v2 ever ships. But on v0's narrow scope, not v2's.
5. Do NOT write any new Butterbase functions. Do NOT touch Substack OAuth. Do NOT book install calls.

**Written follow-up.** One email with the v0-README pasted inline. Bottom line: *"90 days. If something's blocking you, tell me what it is in a named sentence and I'll build it. Otherwise we keep v0 going."*

**Trigger signals (exact phrases).**
- *"Let me just try to write on my own time for a while and see if it sticks."*
- *"I think I'm not ready for any of this — maybe in 6 months."*
- *"Honestly, the more you describe it, the more it sounds like a lot."*

If you haven't, the answer was 'no' and we both saved a bunch of weekends. Either way, no hard feelings and no artifacts sitting around — I'm not filing the §2870 letter, not committing any code. Thedi didn't happen. Cool?"*

**48-hour actions.**
1. Delete the local thedi-v2 branch (keep the research repo, archive the iter1-10 folder as a reference artifact for future projects).
2. Do NOT send any follow-up email referencing Thedi. The silence IS the close.
3. Start the parallel OSS project (same as Branch 3 action 4).

**Written follow-up.** **None.** This is load-bearing. A follow-up email to "confirm we're not doing this" is an invitation to re-open. The absence of follow-up is the confirmation. If Ramesh texts in 2 months asking about it, Ashish replies warmly and offers Branch 3 as the restart path.

## Part 3 — The "oh shit I misread him" recovery protocol

### Direction A — Ashish picked v2; Ramesh was actually Archetype 3 (AI-anxious skeptic)

**Trip-wire.** By week 3 after coffee, one of these specific observables has occurred: (a) Ramesh has asked to audit the critic model's data-handling disclosures before any draft runs; (b) he's rejected the `kimi-k2.5` drafter on model-provenance grounds; (c) he's pushed to add mandatory human-in-the-loop between every rewriter round for the first 10 posts; (d) velocity has dropped to <1 post every 3 weeks because every post has a manual audit checkpoint.

**Course-correction conversation.**
> *"Hey — I want to name something. I think I misread what you wanted at coffee. I heard 'co-design the rubric' and built you a pipeline. What I'm now watching you actually do is audit it, which is different — you're treating it as infrastructure you need to sign off on, not infrastructure you want to tune. That's a legitimate posture but it's a different product. Three options: (1) we keep the pipeline but formalize you as the auditor with mandatory sign-off gates, which slows velocity but matches what you actually want; (2) we pause v2 and fall back to v0 — you write, I edit, no pipeline to audit; (3) we shelve the whole thing. Which feels closest?"*

**Cost of course-correction.** Moderate. The §2870 letter is already filed — but it's accurate, because audit-posture still fits the "OSS maintainer + self-hosting user" framing. Wasted hours: ~20 (installer built, some rubric work) but not the full 80. Social cost: low because Ashish named the misread first, which defuses it.

### Direction B — Ashish picked v0; Ramesh was actually Archetype 5 and feels under-served

**Trip-wire.** By week 6 of v0, one of: (a) Ramesh has shipped 3+ posts AND is asking "so what would the pipeline add?" with specific feature-asks; (b) he's sent a Google Doc with embedded notes like "I'd love it if the system suggested the opening hook"; (c) he's mentioned the pipeline unprompted twice in casual conversation; (d) he's asked to see the iter-5 canonical plan document.

**Course-correction conversation.**
> *"Okay, I think I undersold v2 at coffee because I was worried I was overbuilding. Watching you use v0, I see you'd actually use the pipeline — and you've named three concrete things it would do that v0 doesn't. Let me revise: I'll spend the next 6 weekends building the Phase 1 MVP (interview-bot, rubric, critic, rewriter), you stay on v0 for drafting during the build, and at week 7 we cutover. §2870 letter I'll file this week if you're in. Sound right?"*

**Cost of course-correction.** Low. v0 ran for 6 weeks, shipped real posts, stabilized his voice (which is the iter-6-B cross-archetype amendment anyway — "write 3-5 manual posts first"). The 6 weeks are not wasted; they're the foundation v2 should have had. Actually a *better* state to start v2 from than day-one-v2 would have been. Social cost: near zero, because upgrading is a positive signal ("I was being conservative; you earned the upgrade").

### Asymmetry to internalize

**Direction A (v2→downgrade) costs ~20 hours of sunk work and one awkward conversation. Direction B (v0→upgrade) costs 3 weeks of delay and an easy conversation.** Direction B is strictly cheaper to recover from than Direction A. This asymmetry is why the DECISION.md default is v0, not v2. When in doubt, pick the branch whose misread is cheaper to reverse.

## Part 4 — The one-page phone cheat sheet

*Pull this up in the parking lot 5 minutes before coffee. Don't scroll past the line. Don't try to remember Part 1.*

```
THEDI COFFEE — LISTEN FOR THESE 5 THINGS
(~10% prior on Archetype 5. Your gut will inflate this. Don't.)

1. INITIATIVE
   Did HE bring up Thedi in the last 2 weeks unprompted?
   YES = weighted A5/A2.  NO (only reactive) = A1/A4.

2. SPECIFICITY of wanted outcome
   Can he name "advising WHOM on WHAT"?
   Named = A5/A3.  "You know best" / "both?" = A1/A4.

3. UNPROMPTED questions about rubric/models/prompts
   Does he ask a SECOND follow-up that builds on the first?
   2+ levels deep = A5.  1 question = A3 (audit).  0 = A1/A4.

4. *** THE OVERKILL TEST (tiebreaker) ***
   Say: "Honestly this might be overkill — we could just
   do a Friday digest and a Google Doc."
   Pushback WITH a named specific blocker v0 creates = A5.
   Polite "no no, pipeline sounds cool" = NOT A5. That's politeness.
   Relief = A4/A1. Kill v2.

5. MONEY REACTION (from Act 2 pt 5 in the script)
   Does he name a specific dollar amount to pay you before declining?
   Named amount = A5/A3.  Silent acceptance = A1/A4.

THE ONE QUESTION YOU MUST ASK:
   "If v0 were just a Friday digest and a Google Doc, what
    specifically would it fail to give you?"
   Named specific blocker = Branch 1 (v2).
   "Hmm, I don't know" or vague = Branch 2 (v0 default).
   "Honestly nothing, that sounds great" = Branch 3 (digest only).
   "Actually I think I'll just write on my own" = Branch 4 (drop).

BRANCH DECISION REMINDER:
   5+ signals A5 + named blocker on #4 → v2 from day 1
   3–4 signals A5 → v0 for 3 months (DEFAULT)
   1–2 signals → v0 or digest-only by Signal 2
   0 signals + relief → digest-only
   0 signals + fatigue → drop it

REMEMBER: misreading toward v0 costs 3 weeks. Misreading
toward v2 costs 80 hours and a filed letter. When unsure,
pick v0. You can always upgrade. You can't un-file a letter
cleanly.
```
