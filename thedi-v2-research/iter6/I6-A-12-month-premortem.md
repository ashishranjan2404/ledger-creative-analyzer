## Month 1 — 2026-04 (pre-launch; paper artifacts; coffee conversation)

**Success path.** Ashish lands in California, starts at Saviynt on 2026-04-27, and — critically — has a 30-minute coffee with Ramesh in the week of 2026-04-20 *before* the §2870 letter is filed. Per the iter-4 "what I would actually do" paragraph, coffee runs ahead of legal. Artifact 1 (the §2870 letter) is filled in to the boundary where the attorney's pressure-test is the only remaining dependency. By Saturday 2026-04-25, dry-run #1 of the installer against a throwaway Butterbase account completes D1–D11 per the I3-A checklist. Ramesh has spent ~45 minutes: one coffee, one exchange of texts confirming a preferred time.

**Failure path.** The most plausible failure this month is that **the coffee conversation doesn't happen, or happens but is non-committal**. Ramesh is mid-quarter at Saviynt with his own deadlines; "let's grab coffee next week" slides to the week after. Ashish, feeling the clock of his 2026-04-27 start date, files the §2870 letter anyway on the assumption that Ramesh will ratify. This is exactly the sequence iter-4's Risk 4 warns about: the letter's scope locks before the North Star is picked, and the legal artifact then carves out a version of Thedi that Ramesh's actual answer renders overbuilt. A secondary, quieter failure: the attorney reply lands from a small firm that quotes 2 weeks out, and by the time Ashish integrates their edits it's the week of his Saviynt start, when evening hours are evaporated by onboarding. Artifact 1 ships on 2026-04-27 with `[verify with attorney]` tags still in it. Nothing visible breaks; the "attack surface on §2870(a)(1) adjacency" is simply not pressure-tested.

**Decision / review point.** Ashish evaluates whether the coffee happened AND whether Ramesh named a North Star in his own words (not a reformulation Ashish imposed). Ramesh evaluates whether the ask feels reasonable — does he actually want this thing, or is he being polite? The pipeline has nothing to auto-evaluate; there is no pipeline. The single trip-wire available is the I3-A tracker doc's Week-0 gates: attorney consult booked, §2870 letter finalized, moonlighting disclosure filed.

**Branch point.** If Ramesh's real answer at coffee is *"honestly, just send me a Friday digest — I don't need a pipeline,"* the iter-5 canonical Executive Summary explicitly flags this as ~40% likely and "the attorney fee, the letter filing, and seven weekend blocks are all sunk." That is the revisit moment. The §2870 letter should still file (it protects prior-art-by-Ashish for v1 anyway) but Phase 1 tasks 5–7 (interview-bot MVP, pgvector dedup, `/admin` dashboard) pause. Ashish ships a v0.3 scout-only Friday digest instead. Everything else compresses to ~4 weekend hours total and the 12-month arc collapses to a much cheaper plan.

## Month 3 — 2026-06 (first pipeline post or first manual post)

**Success path (B2-conservative branch).** Ramesh drafts post 1 in Bear, pastes it into `/admin/compose` with `source='manual_paste_for_calibration'` per iter-4 Risk 1 mitigation, clicks Approve, pastes to Substack. The pipeline is idle — but the synthetic end-to-end post button on `/admin/pipeline` (iter-4 Risk 3 mitigation) fires every 7 days against canned topic + canned Q&A, exercising every real API so nothing rots silently. Three manual posts ship in month 3.

**Failure path.** The most dangerous month-3 failure is **silent voice-preservation failure**. Under B2-aggressive, post 1 ships and Ramesh's public voice is measurably his — but the critic's `voice_fidelity` score is calibrated to the seed interview's Q&A register, not Ramesh's essay register. Ramesh reads post 1, thinks "huh, not quite how I'd say it, but fine" and approves. Post 2 lands with a similar delta. Neither is bad enough to reject. The authorship paper (arXiv 2509.14543) capped blog-register imitation at 19–65%; the canonical plan accepts this is the failure surface the entire voice-anchoring architecture was designed to defend against. The failure signal would be Ramesh saying "fine" five weeks in a row. Secondary failure under B2-conservative: Ramesh writes post 1 in Bear, pastes to Substack directly bypassing `/admin/compose` entirely (the iter-4 Risk 1 "empty stage" scenario). The silence-counter at `/admin` hits 14 days, the pipeline auto-pauses, Ashish gets the alert, replies "ignore — he's writing manually," and the trip-wire gets desensitized on its first real test. Tertiary: an IonRouter model alias change between install-day smoke-test and first real call — caught by the model-ID assertion iff the assertion actually shipped before the drafter ran.

**Decision / review point.** Ashish evaluates: did the first post's `voice_fidelity` rubric score agree with Ramesh's subjective gut? The calibration gap is the whole premise. Ramesh evaluates: does reading his own byline feel right? (The single most load-bearing judgment he'll make all year.) The pipeline auto-evaluates: model-ID assertion pass rate, critic weighted score, rewriter-round count, IonRouter spend, `/admin` silence counter. First weekly health email goes out 2026-06-15; first meaningful reading of trend data is possible after 3 weeks.

**Branch point.** If Ramesh says post 1 reads *"a bit off, I can't quite say why"*: **pause the pipeline.** Run the cold-start seed interview again with deeper cadence-marker extraction. Consider a 2-round pre-publish critique loop (above the 2-round Self-Refine cap) for posts 2–5, accepting the quality-plateau tradeoff. If `voice_fidelity` rubric scores trend downward 3 weeks in (voice-drift detector fires: rolling 3-post mean drops >1pt vs. prior 8-post mean), revisit the drafter model — possibly swap to Claude Sonnet at 5× cost, accepting the budget hit in exchange for the canonical plan's North Star survival. If B2-conservative and Ramesh bypasses `/admin` entirely, the 30-day silence threshold (raised per iter-4 Risk 1) holds through month 3, but the rubric has zero Ramesh-edit signal and the month-5 transition to pipeline-primary is now an expensive cliff.
