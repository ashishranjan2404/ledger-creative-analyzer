## Why this project exists

This repo is the D2 precondition from the Thedi v2 research package (iter-6 pre-mortem, amendment D2). Thedi v2 is a real project, but from a career-narrative standpoint it is built-for-Ramesh: the pinned-repo signal reads "helpful engineering for a colleague's Substack," not "this person thinks at the platform/agentic-AI layer." A solo Thedi pinning is a month-12 audit failure regardless of whether Thedi itself ships successfully. `agentspy` is the structural answer: a second OSS artifact on the personal GitHub whose story is fully yours and whose domain (agent observability / dev tooling) is squarely agentic-AI-platform.

Per I10-B's analysis, `agentspy` won on four axes simultaneously: high pinned-repo signal, sustainable at 2h/wk, zero Saviynt adjacency, and low Thedi overlap. The critical self-feeding property: every trace generated by debugging Thedi is a test fixture for `agentspy`. The two projects compound rather than compete for the same hours.

## The 2h/wk budget and its rhythm

Hard ceiling: two hours per week, measured as a rolling 4-week average. The natural cadence is one Sunday-evening sitting or one weeknight block. If a week is zero hours that is fine; if a week is four hours the next two must be zero.

Roadmap pacing (from `docs/ROADMAP.md`):
- Weeks 1–2: schema + scaffolding.
- Weeks 3–5: parser.
- Week 6: fixtures.
- Weeks 7–10: TUI renderer.
- Week 10 end: tag v0.1, publish to npm, flip public.

If week 10 is actually week 16, that is within tolerance. The rhythm matters more than any individual milestone.

## §2870 safety as a positive design constraint

`agentspy` is local-first and has no auth because those are product differentiators in its category. They are also, independently, the properties that keep it structurally clear of any IAM / IGA / PAM territory. A viewer that cannot reach a network and cannot authenticate anyone cannot drift into identity-management adjacency no matter how scope creep pressure accumulates.

Treat the no-network and no-auth invariants as non-negotiable. If a contributor proposes a feature that requires either (a remote sharing feature, a team view, a SaaS dashboard mirror), decline. The §2870 carve-out protection and the product's differentiation sit on the same property; preserving one preserves the other at no cost.

## When to pause this project

Hard trigger: if Thedi v2 actually gets built — i.e., Ramesh signals unambiguous scope-5 archetype at coffee, the §2870 letter files clean, and Ashish is in fact spending 4+ hours a week on Thedi build work — `agentspy` deprioritizes. In that state, Thedi itself carries enough career-narrative weight to make `agentspy` a nice-to-have rather than a structural requirement. Slow the cadence to "ship patches when patches are owed" and stop opening new feature issues.

Soft trigger: if three consecutive months clock <30 minutes on `agentspy` and the reason is not Thedi build pressure but simply low interest, archive it publicly with a one-sentence note and move on. A dormant repo with a clear README and an honest status line is a better career artifact than a stale-but-propped-up one.

Do not pause this project because Thedi is stressful. Stress on Thedi is the exact condition under which `agentspy` earns its keep — debugging Thedi traces is `agentspy`'s use case, and the ≤2h/wk ceiling is deliberately low enough to not compete with Thedi's weekend hours.
